Mars \n Mars is the fourth planet from the sun. \n The surface of Mars is orange red because it is covered in iron (III) oxide dust, giving it the nickname the Red Planet  .\n Mars was formed approximately 4.5 billion years ago. \n Mars is a terrestrial planet with a surface that consists of minerals containing silicon and oxygen, metals, and other elements that typically make up rock. \n Mars is about half the diameter of Earth, with a surface area only slightly less than Earth's dry land's total area. \n Of all the planets in the Solar System, the seasons of Mars are the most Earth like, due to the similar tilts of the two planets' rotational axes. \n Mars has a gravitational force of 3.71 m\s to the power of 2. \n Mythology \n In ancient Roman religion and mythology, Mars is the god of war and also an agricultural guardian, a combination characteristic of early Rome. \n He is the son of Jupiter and Juno, and was pre eminent among the Roman army's military gods. \n Most of his festivals were held in March, the month named for him (Latin Martius), and in October, the months which traditionally began and ended the season for both military campaigning and farming.\n \n Atmosphere composition \n 95.72percent carbon dioxide; 2.7percent nitrogen; 1.6percent argon; 0.2percent oxygen; carbon monoxide 0.07percent; \n 0.03percent water vapor; 0.01percent nitric oxide; 2.5 ppm neon; 300 ppb krypton; 30 ppb formaldehyde; \n 80 ppb xenon; 30 ppb ozone, 10 ppb methane.
